## In players.db you’ll find a single table, players. In the players table, you’ll find the following columns:

1. id, which uniquely identifies each row (player) in the table
2. first_name, which is the first name of the player
3. last_name, which is the last name of the player
4. bats, which is the side (R for right or L for left) the player bats on
5. throws, which is the hand (R for right or L for left) the player throws with
6. weight, which is the player’s weight in pounds
7. height, which is the player’s height in inches
8. debut, which is the date (expressed as YYYY-MM-DD) the player began their career in the MLB
9. final_game, which is the date (expressed as YYYY-MM-DD) the player played their last game in the MLB
10. birth_year, which is the year the player was born
11. birth_month, which is the month (expressed as an integer) the player was born
12. birth_day, which is the day the player was born
13. birth_city, which is the city in which the player was born
14. birth_state, which is the state in which the player was born
15. birth_country, which is the country in which the player was born
